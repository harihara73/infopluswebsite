Human Anatomy and Physiology

The adult human skull is comprised of twenty-two bones
which are divided into two groups: Cranial bones and facial bones.
The Rib cage surrounds and protects the heart and lungs in the thoracic cavity.
It consists of the sternum, ribs and the thoracic vertebrae, to which the ribs articulate.
The heart is a cone-shaped hollow muscular organ located
within the thoracic cavity, medially between the lungs in the space known as mediastinum.
The broad upper part of the heart is known as the base and the lower narrow part is known as the apex.
The brain is made up of three main parts, cerebrum, cerebellum and medulla oblongata.
Cerebrum is the largest part of the brain and is composed of right and left hemispheres.
A neuron is a structural and functional unit of the brain.
They communicate by sending nerve impulses to the central nervous system which in turn processed
and sent to the parts of the body for quick action. Nerve impulses travel a great distance
at a speed ranging from 0.5 to 130 meters/second.
The ovaries are the female gonads that produce the ovum.
Two ovaries each about 2 to 3 cm in length, about the size of an almond lie in shallow depressions,
called ovarian fossa, one on each side of the uterus, in the lateral walls of the pelvic cavity.
